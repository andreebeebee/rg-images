Most marketers chase splashy visuals and trend-of-the-week aesthetics. Mike Nelson shows why email design, done right, can be even more powerful.

In this engaging session, Mike moves from brand fundamentals to visual language and into real-world email design trends that drive results. He breaks down why a logo is not a brand, how consistent experiences in the inbox shape perception, and which trends actually help users see, read, and act. From IKEA’s instantly recognizable system to practical tactics like deep and dark palettes for dark mode, color blocking for hierarchy, raw and unfiltered imagery, nostalgic web motifs, and ticker GIFs for urgency, Mike explains what works, what does not, and most importantly, why.

This is not about spamming inboxes. It is about treating email as a brand experience channel, using clear typographic hierarchy, accessible color contrast, and purposeful imagery to create relevance, consistency, and measurable engagement.

TL;DR:

  • Brand > logo: consistent inbox experiences build memory and trust
  • Clarity beats clutter: color blocking, hierarchy, and readable type win
  • Design with context: dark mode friendly palettes outperform pastel overload
  • Real > polished: raw photos and honest visuals increase authenticity
  • Nostalgia, used wisely: retro cues can boost attention when they aid comprehension
  • Motion with a job: ticker GIFs create urgency only if they stay accessible
